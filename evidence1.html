Evidence #1: The Preamble to the Constitution

How does does the Preamble support my answer?



The Preamble states the purpose for creating the Constitution. It’s relevant to the question because it doesn’t agree with the suspension of Habeas Corpus. Habeas Corpus literally translates to “bring back the body”, or to liberate someone who was wrongfully imprisoned. If the Government is able to suspend this right, they would be going against the whole purpose of the Government- to “establish justice” and “secure the Blessings of Liberty to ourselves”. That is why the Government should never be able to suspend this crucial right.
